Skinny Pizza Dough

Original Skinny Pizza Dough

Ingredients

2 cups self-rising flour

1 teaspoon yeast

1 teaspoon salt

1/2 cup nonfat Greek yogurt

1/2 to 3/4 cups water

Method

Add flour, yeast, and salt to a large bowl. Mix with a fork.

Add the yogurt and mix until combined.

Add just under 1/2 cup water to start, increasing only to get the dough together. You may find you’ll need more (or less) water, so adjust as needed.

Once combined, store it in the microwave, covered, and let rise 12 hours. The dough is best after day 2 or 3 and lasts in the fridge for up to 7 days.

Gluten Free Skinny Pizza Dough

Need a version without flour? No worries, I’ve got you covered! I like using Bob’s Red Mill Gluten Free 1-to-1 Baking Flour, but feel free to substitute your own.

Ingredients

2-1/4 cups Bob’s Red Mill 1-to-1 baking flour or GF baking flour

1-1/2 teaspoons yeast

1 teaspoon salt

1/4 cup cornmeal

1 teaspoon sugar

1/2 to 3/4 cups water

1 tablespoon butter, softened

Method

In a large bowl, mix 1/2 cup water, yeast, and sugar together. Stir and set aside for 10 minutes.

Mix in the flour, cornmeal, and salt. Mix well. Add only enough water (tablespoon at a time) until the dough comes together.

Cover and let rise one hour.

Roll out dough. Spread the butter on the dough and knead for five minutes. Separate dough into two crusts and refrigerate until ready to use.
