The Story

I’ve always loved being in the kitchen. However, when my daughter Hannah was little, we typically only ate about five dishes: pizza, hot dogs, chicken fingers, pasta, and tacos!

So when I met and married Jeff, my late husband, I realized I needed to step up my game in the kitchen. I was 32 and ready to move beyond those five staples.

How did I learn how to cook? By cooking! It’s really the only way that you can hone your skill and gain instincts in the kitchen.

I was notorious for following a recipe to a T, never tasting anything along the way, and putting the dishes on the table expecting them to be perfect.

However, after the first bite I would say “Dang, I wish I would have added crushed red pepper or more salt.” It was then that I learned that you simply must taste every element of the dish before it hits the dinner table.

I also learned to love more vegetables than I ever have in my life. I believe that vegetables I ate growing up were either canned or overcooked and I relied on cucumbers and carrots as my go to vegetables. So if you are like me, I’d suggest trying some vegetables again that you’ve given the stink eye too. I didn’t fall in love with mushrooms until I was 45!

Please enjoy this labor of love. I continually had you all in mind as I was creating these dishes and I can’t wait to hear what you think.

While 90% of these recipes are made for one, I did add a few recipes that made sense to make more than one serving, such as my buffalo chicken chili – I could eat that twice in a week and freeze the leftovers to reheat on nights when I am short on time.
